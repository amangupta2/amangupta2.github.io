I study the impact of climate change on the large-scale atmospheric circulation and mesoscale atmospheric variability using a combination of theory, modeling, and observations.

I am a postdoctoral research at Stanford's Doerr School of Sustainability where I am developing machine learning-based climate model parameterizations to represent sub-grid scale atmospheric processes, like gravity waves. Prior to this, I worked at the Ludwig-Maximilian University of Munich (LMU), in collaboration with the German Aerospace Center (DLR), where I investigated the role of gravity waves in driving the stratospheric circulation (the Brewer-Dobson circulation), and assessed the momentum fluxes due to gravity waves generated over South American Andes.

I received my Ph.D. from the Center for Atmosphere Ocean Science, at the Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences, New York University, where my research focused on assessing stratosphere-troposphere dynamical coupling & tracer transport in idealized climate models, and implications of model transport biases for projections of the Antarctic ozone hole recovery.
