Aman Gupta

Welcome to my webspace. I am a fourth year Ph.D. student in the Center for Atmosphere Ocean Sciences, at the Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences, NYU. My advisor is Edwin Gerber.

My research, lying at the intersection of computational mathematics, high performance computing and climate dynamics, focuses on developing metrics to assess Tracer Transport and Stratosphere Dynamics in GCMs on climatological timescales. I use dynamical cores developed at Princeton-GFDL(AM3) and NCAR(CAM5), using a wide range of numerics; The philosophical goal being - to bridge the gap between Modeling, Observations and Theory.

More generally, I am also interested in studying the atmospheres of extra-terrestrial planets and the dynamical properties and turbulent nature of such fluid flows.

My email : agupta [at] cims [dot] nyu [dot] edu

Research

Connecting Stratospheric Circulation and Tracer Transport in GCMs

Reducing tracer transport biases due to numerics among atmospheric dynamical cores & tracer based measurements, and creating tracer transport benchmarks for atmospheric dynamical cores. Connecting Theory, Models and Observations.

Isentropic Circulation in GCMs

Techniques in Dry Isentropic Analysis of Global Circulation in GCMs. Assesing a set of techniques to compute mass transport and TEM in a generalized(Isentropic and Age-based) coordinate system.
